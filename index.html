Data Handling

Data handling is a crucial part of data science that involves collecting, cleaning, organizing, and storing data in a way that makes it ready for analysis. Since raw data often comes in messy or incomplete forms, data scientists must first preprocess it — removing duplicates, handling missing values, and converting it into usable formats. Efficient data handling ensures better model performance, accurate insights, and faster decision-making. It forms the foundation for all further steps in data science, including data visualization, machine learning, and statistical analysis.

Data Cleaning

Data cleaning is a vital step in data science that involves fixing or removing incorrect, incomplete, or irrelevant data from a dataset. Real-world data is often messy—it may contain missing values, duplicates, errors, or inconsistent formats. Cleaning the data helps improve the quality and accuracy of the analysis. This process includes tasks like filling in missing values, correcting data types, removing outliers, and standardizing formats. Without proper data cleaning, even the most advanced models can give misleading results.
